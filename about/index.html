About

I first decided to pursue a career in physics at the age of 13, due in part to the influence of a fantastic high school teacher who gave me my first glimpse into the beauty of the physical world. This decision has since carried me through an undergraduate physics degree from Swarthmore College (oh-leven!), a National Science Foundation Graduate Student Research Fellowship, and a Master's and PhD in physics from UC Santa Cruz. I just recently finished up a postdoctoral research position at UC Santa Cruz, and after realizing that some decisions are best not left up to 13-year-olds, have decided to use my analytical skills for other purposes, and am currently the Director of Operations for CleanRobotics. While I'm not working, you can find me hiking, biking, and discussing the plight of science in politics over a craft beer.
